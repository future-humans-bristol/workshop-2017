About

The Future Humans Workshop is a unique opportunity for Bristol postgraduate researchers from across the University to collaborate on the topic of humanity's future relationship with technology.

Where will our relationship with technology take us?

Workshop 2017

AI for the home / Prosthetics / Cultural evolution / Driverless cars / Living with technology / Tactile robotics / Orthopaedic implants / Transhumanism / Virtual reality / Drug delivery / Legal challenges / Impact on infrastructure

The list goes on.

If your research involves thinking about how the future of technology will shape us, or how our future will influence technology, this workshop is aimed at you.

The Future Humans Workshop is a one-day event designed to let participants develop their ideas with others and help shape them. We're leading specially designed exercises, prompts, and space for you to turn your ideas into mini projects.

Be inspired, share ideas, and collaborate with other researchers on the topic of the human-technological future.


The Future Humans Workshop will provide the opportunity to refine and present your PhD ideas to a new crowd. Those who want to take their shared ideas further will be given the opportunity to participate as a group at the University of Bristol Research Without Borders Festival showcase exhibition. Academics and industry partners will all be attending the exhibition, so why not give it a go?
